I grew up in one of coldest part of the Globe - Western Siberia, so when I moved to the USA, I did not want to ever see snow again, that is how I ended up moving to San Francisco. Since my current work allows me to do it remotely, I am away for at least 3 months every year. But every time I come back to San Francisco, I know that this place is my favorite.

Several years ago I decided to possibly switch a career from Finance to Computer Science. CCSF was very helpful in that since I could take lots of online courses. I have already gained several Certificates of Achievement such as Lunux Administration, Devops, and Data Science. I think I would also like to recevie Certificate in Frond End Development. I really enjoyed Python, Linux, and Data Visualization classes, and I am looking forward to taking some more courses that would allow to focus more on front end side. I see myself to eventually incorporating all skills I have achieved in order to get a position of a full stack developer or similar position.

Good links

  • StackOverflow is one of the most resourceful platform as you will literally find anything there, almost every single issue I have had during my studies I was able to find helpful information in that portal.
  • FreeCodeCamp is probably one of the best free resources on the internet to learn web development. It also teaches you everything about responsive web design. The website offers you hundreds of projects and exercises to test your knowledge.
  • Coursera is a popular online learning platform where all the courses are free. It was founded by Stanford professors Daphne Koller and Andrew Ng. Coursera works with the top educational institutions in the world that provide over 1000 courses to Coursera.
